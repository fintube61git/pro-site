External tools for understanding internal systems

Some inner experiences are best met one part at a time. Others only make sense when seen as a system.

These tools are for individual, self-directed exploration. They are not therapy, diagnosis, or clinical guidance.

They can also support collaboration in treatment: you can share what you map or journal with your therapist, and therapists may invite clients to use them between sessions.

These web apps do not collect personal data or usage telemetry. Your work is not sent to a server, and you only save or export anything if you choose to do so on your own device.

They are free and web-based. For clinical care, see Contact. For data handling details, see the Privacy Policy.

Get to know the parts

ip-journal.live

Parts Journal supports moment-to-moment contact with what is present right now.

Using image prompts, the 6 Fs, and a brief Self-energy check, the journal helps slow attention and support unblending. Parts can be noticed as living experiences rather than analyzed or managed.

The journal does not interpret or direct outcomes. It provides structure for noticing and relating.

Privacy by default: no telemetry is collected, and any saved work remains under your control on your device.

Use the journal when:

  • something feels activated or unclear
  • you want to slow down and listen
  • the goal is contact, not explanation
Visit ip-journal.live

Understand the system

ip-mapper.com

Parts Mapper supports seeing how internal parts relate across time.

It lets you externalize an internal system: naming parts, mapping relationships, and observing patterns or polarizations that emerge beyond any single moment.

The mapper does not diagnose or prescribe. It offers structure and visibility so patterns can be observed rather than inferred.

Privacy by default: no telemetry is collected, and any saved work remains under your control on your device.

Use the mapper when:

  • the same dynamics repeat
  • multiple parts are involved
  • you want a system-level view
Visit ip-mapper.com
